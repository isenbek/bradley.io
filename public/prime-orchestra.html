OPERATION — Drag the slider (or press RESOLVE) to add zeta-zero waves to the smooth axis one instrument at a time. Each wave is 2√x·cos(γ·log x − α)/|ρ| — featureless alone. Watch the interference sharpen into vertical jumps at exactly the primes and prime powers (each jump has height log p). CH·2 shows the single wave you just added: a stretching chirp in linear x — hit LOG X and it snaps into a pure sinusoid, the primes' native coordinate system. Staircase teeth at 2, 3, 5, 7, 11… emerge from waves that individually know nothing. CH·3 tracks the reconstruction's y-value at one probe point x₀ (crosshair — click anywhere on CH·1 to move it) as a function of wave count N. The amber trace is the partial sum hunting the true value (dashed); the dim tail is its future. Probe mid-gap and it settles fast; probe just next to a prime and watch it ring — each wave overshoots and corrects, the Gibbs struggle at a discontinuity rendered as a time series. CH·4 replots the same trace as a lag-1 return map: each point is (ψN−1, ψN). The dashed diagonal is y = x; the circled dot is the fixed point (true ψ, true ψ). Convergence appears as an orbit spiraling into the fixed point — points hugging the diagonal mean consecutive sums barely changed, wide loops mean a big-amplitude wave just joined. Move the probe and watch the orbit's geometry change character.
